## Project Overview

An online multiple platformer racing game created for Florida Poly's Game Development 2 course and
presented to the public at the university's Spring 2023 Game Expo.

Players compete against each other for the highest score by jumping to the goal. The finished
product was published on itch.io as a public release.

## Technologies Used

- [Unity](https://unity.com/)
- [Aseprite](https://www.aseprite.org/)

## What I Learned

Every part of this project was created by me with the exceptions of sound assets and the network
engine.

Games are notorious for being difficult to apply the SDLC, which is exactly why my professor
enforced the SDLC, not allowing us to start writing code until we had documented requirements, uses
cases, object diagrams, and more.

I am by no means an artistic person. I could have used free asset packs for my game's art, but then
I felt that would be too limiting so I challenged myself by creating all the art myself. To keep
things simple, I chose to use pixel art no bigger than 16x16 and limited myself to a handful of
colors reminiscent of the original Game Boy. Considering I had barely touched pixel art before this,
I'm fairly proud of the result.

In most school projects, the result is usually presented to professors and peers. My professor made
it a requirement to present our games at the Game Expo, a semi-annual event open to the public. Each
team was given a space to prepare and manage however they wanted. Admittedly, I was short on time
after finding some critical bugs. I managed to put together a poster and folded a number of paper
jumping frogs to give away to players who achieved first place in the game.

Evidently, not many people actually reached the goal. The game was originally designed to be a bit
difficult due to its unforgiving nature - think missing a platform in Jump King and falling back to
the start, just on a smaller scale. That difficulty increased to near impossible due to the network
latency we experienced that day. Luckily, people still enjoyed playing and virtually all of them had
positive feedback.
